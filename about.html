About This Project

How a celebration of life became a bridge across generations

The Archive

The family archive now contains over 1,700 indexed documents spanning 1833 to 2022:

  • Handwritten letters between siblings, parents, and children
  • Typed correspondence and carbon copies from the mid-20th century
  • Legal documents, land records, and genealogical papers
  • Bill Gabriel’s memoir, Memories of a Career
  • Julian Kenneth Hickman’s political papers and broadcasts
  • Hundreds of photographs from the 1880s through the 2000s

78 items have been flagged as “notable correspondence” — letters that reveal character, mark turning points, or capture moments of extraordinary family connection.

The Mission: Steward & Tell

This project has two purposes:

Steward — Preserve, organize, and protect the family archive so that nothing is lost. Every letter, every photograph, every document is being cataloged, digitized, and made accessible.

Tell — Share the stories. The archive proves that staying connected is possible. The website is designed to make the family’s history not just stored but alive — something you can explore, contribute to, and pass forward.

The previous generation wrote letters. This generation has a website, a comment section, and an open invitation to add their own stories to the record.

The Website

This site — hickmansofbackcreek.org — was built in February 2026 as a single-page family tree and archive explorer. It includes:

  • Interactive profiles for over 38 family members, each with biographies, letters, and photographs
  • A timeline spanning 10 generations from the 1700s to the present
  • An interactive map showing where the family has lived
  • Searchable photo archives, including digitized vintage photographs
  • A contribution form for family members to submit corrections, stories, and photos

The site is maintained by Peter Lightner Hickman II, with help from family members and the growing archive. It is a living project — always being updated, always open to contributions.

If you have family papers, photos, corrections, or memories to share, please visit the Contribute section.
